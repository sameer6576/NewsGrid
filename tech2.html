Hacker claims to have stolen 1 billion records of Chinese citizens from police

Written By Sameer Ahmed July 5,2022
Technology

An anonymous hacker has claimed to have stolen data on as many as a billion Chinese residents after breaching a database from the Shanghai police, according to multiple reports. The incident, if real, would be “one of the biggest data breaches in the history,” said experts. The anonymous Internet user, identified as ChinaDan, posted on hacker forum Breach Forums last week offering to sell the more than 23 terabytes (TB) of data for 10 bitcoins, equivalent to about $200,000.


"In 2022, the Shanghai National Police (SHGA) database was leaked. This database contains many terabytes of data and information on billions of Chinese citizens," the post said. "Databases contain information on 1 billion Chinese national residents and several billion case records, including: Name, address, birthplace, national ID number, mobile number, all crime and case details." The Shanghai government and police department did not respond to requests for comment until now.
